# Warm redesign review notes

## Visual review — hero and upper sections

The updated MindPro design is visibly warmer and more inviting than the previous iteration. The shift from colder emerald highlights to an olive-sand palette works well and preserves a premium tone. The official MindPro logo is now integrated into the header and hero card, which strengthens branding and makes the site feel more finished and specific.

### What is working well

| Area | Finding |
| --- | --- |
| Brand presence | The official logo improves credibility and gives the header a more polished identity than the text-only badge. |
| Color direction | The olive-charcoal background with sand and sage accents feels warmer, more therapeutic, and still professional. |
| Typography | The serif headline plus cleaner sans-serif body pairing feels more editorial and less generic. |
| Hero composition | The hero remains strong and premium while feeling less harsh than the previous darker version. |
| Mid-page cards | The softer borders and warm surface tones make the cards feel more welcoming and less cold. |

### Details observed visually

The header still reads clearly against the dark warm background and the CTA remains visible. The main headline is elegant and readable in Hebrew, and the section introducing the three audience groups has improved warmth without losing structure. The consultation image section feels more aligned with a psychology-and-performance brand than with a purely athletic campaign aesthetic.

### Potential refinement to check later

The top navigation appears slightly small and faint against the background at this zoom level, so it may benefit from a little more contrast or weight if needed after full-page review. This is a refinement note, not a blocking issue.
