# MindPro design direction notes

The next iteration should move the site away from a colder, high-contrast athletic look and toward a **warmer premium clinical-wellness direction**. The goal is to keep authority and performance energy, but make the experience feel more human, approachable, and emotionally safe.

The official MindPro logo has been located in the Drive folder and prepared for web use. The logo asset is available as a CDN-hosted SVG and should replace the temporary text-only brand treatment in the header and branded areas.

The color guidance gathered for therapist and wellness brands points to a more balanced palette built from **greens, neutrals, and warm earthy tones** rather than purely cold dark greens. The therapist website guidance emphasizes that blues and greens are effective because they communicate calm, trust, health, and tranquility. The wellness branding reference further suggests that **sage, sand, clay, warm taupe, champagne, muted gold, and soft amber accents** create a warmer and more elevated feeling while remaining professional.

For MindPro, the applied palette direction should therefore be:

| Role | Direction |
| --- | --- |
| Base background | Deep olive-charcoal rather than near-black |
| Secondary surfaces | Warm forest, moss, or taupe-tinted dark panels |
| Main accent | Soft sage-mint rather than neon mint |
| Warm support accent | Champagne / muted gold / light sand |
| Text color | Warm off-white instead of stark white |
| Borders and overlays | Bronze-taupe or olive-tinted translucent lines |

Typography should also become more refined and recognizably editorial. The best direction is to introduce a **Hebrew serif for headings** to add warmth and distinction, paired with a highly readable **modern Hebrew sans-serif for body copy and interface text**. The intended pairing for implementation is a premium serif-led hierarchy with a cleaner sans-serif companion so the site feels both authoritative and inviting.

The redesign should specifically improve four visual areas. First, the header should include the real MindPro logo and feel less generic. Second, the hero should gain warmer overlays and softer contrast so the photography feels more welcoming. Third, cards and sections should use more layered tonal variation instead of one dominant dark value. Fourth, the typography scale should feel more premium, with a clearer distinction between editorial headlines and supporting explanatory text.

The final outcome should feel closer to **premium wellness meets performance psychology** rather than **dark athletic campaign page**.
